This page documents every technique in the Khazix style library with real, verbatim examples. Use it as a lookup when writing or reviewing articles. The examples are in Chinese — that is intentional; the style is built in that language.

<Info>
  These are not templates to copy verbatim. They are anchors that show what each technique looks and feels like. Read them, internalize the pattern, then express it in your own words.
</Info>

## 1. Opening examples

The opening never starts with grand proclamations. It drops you into a specific, present-tense moment and makes you ask "then what?"

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Narrative startup — 叙事启动型">
    **Technique:** Open with "故事是这样的" (the story goes like this), then immediately zoom into a mundane, specific detail. The mundaneness is the point — it signals that this is real life, not a lecture.

    > 故事是这样的。
    > 我最近刷淘宝挺多，然后昨天，鬼使神差的，在淘宝上，搜了一下DeepSeek。

    > 故事是这样的。
    > 是周末跟一个刚毕业两三年的朋友聊天，聊到了天赋这个词。

    **What makes it work:** "鬼使神差的" (inexplicably, on a whim) immediately signals honest, unplanned discovery. The reader knows this isn't going to be a polished essay — it's going to be a real experience.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Absurd fact — 荒诞事实型">
    **Technique:** Open with a statement so surprising or absurd it forces the reader to double-take. Follow it immediately with a deadpan punchline.

    > 我可能，刚刚成为了哈基米的儿子。
    > 至少，AI是这么认为的。

    > 这个世界，终于魔幻到我看不懂的程度了。

    **What makes it work:** "我可能" (I might have) softens the absurdity just enough to be believable. The follow-up "至少，AI是这么认为的" (at least, that's what AI thinks) delivers the punchline without explaining it — the reader's curiosity has to do the rest.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Trending topic — 热点破题型">
    **Technique:** Name the trending thing, but immediately attach a personal entry point rather than describing the trend abstractly.

    > 最近这两天，被一个三宫格AI图片给刷屏了。

    > Seedance2.0的火，已经烧了好几天了。
    > 抖音b站到处都是二创视频，微博科技榜一下子上了四个热搜。

    **What makes it work:** The second example accumulates evidence — Douyin, Bilibili, Weibo trending — in a single breath. It shows the writer has been living in this, not observing from a distance.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Curiosity-driven — 好奇心驱动型">
    **Technique:** Tease something interesting without naming it yet. The reader is pulled forward by wanting to see what "the thing" actually is.

    > 这两天在网上刷到了一张图，很有意思。

    > 最近关于OpenClaw的事，除了我昨天说的Github登顶之外。
    > 还有另一个非常魔幻的事。

    **What makes it work:** "还有另一个非常魔幻的事" (there's another completely surreal thing) is a commitment — the writer is promising a payoff. The reader has to keep reading to collect on it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sensory event — 感性事件型">
    **Technique:** Ground the reader in a physical moment — day, time, activity — before the news or topic arrives.

    > 大周末的，起床第一件事，就看到，飞书开源CLI了。。。

    > 前天，我们终于开了属于我们自己公司的年会。

    **What makes it work:** "大周末的" (on a weekend, of all times) + "起床第一件事" (the very first thing after waking up) paints a relatable scene before any information arrives. The "。。。" trails off in mock exhaustion, which is itself a mood.

    <Note>
      Pattern summary: every opening type cuts into a specific, current event. There is no broad-strokes scene-setting. The first sentence creates the urge to ask "then what?"
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 2. Colloquial transitions

Transitions carry the reader from one thought to the next without making the seam visible. Bad transitions announce themselves; good ones feel like the writer just remembered something.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Good transitions — 好的转场">
    **Technique:** Use spoken-word pivots that imply a thinking, present human rather than a structured document.

    - "说实话，一年前我根本想不到今天会是这个样子。"
    - "反正我看得时候，脑子里有两个想法。"
    - "这话听着有点刺耳，但我有时候觉得，他说的确实就是事实。"
    - "顺着上面的，再聊聊岗位。"
    - "说到AI工具，我必须聊一个我自己感触特别深的东西。"
    - "回到上门安装OpenClaw这块，这块的安全风险，就真的更大了。"
    - "这块需要注意一下，我不是贬低这些业务，有需求就有市场，这个更正常。"
    - "当然，一切的调研，还是要躬身入局一下比较好。"
    - "米家说完了，再用一点点篇幅，来说一下另一个稍微我觉得有点用的功能。"
    - "我突然想到了我的父亲。"
    - "写着写着，突然感觉回到了7、8年前还在做用户体验设计的时候。"
    - "让我们回到最开始的爱心。"
    - "想了想，突然想起了十年前。"

    **What makes them work:** Many of these simulate a mid-thought interruption or a spontaneous memory. "我突然想到了我的父亲" has nothing to do with AI — and that's exactly why it works. The unpredictability is the proof of a real person thinking in real time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Transitions to avoid — 要避免的转场">
    **These signal AI authorship immediately:**

    - "接下来，让我们讨论..."
    - "值得注意的是..."
    - "在深入探讨之前，我们需要先了解..."

    <Warning>
      "值得注意的是" (it is worth noting that) and "接下来让我们" (next, let us) are among the most reliable AI fingerprints. Their appearance instantly breaks the spoken-word illusion.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 3. Knowledge "mentioned in passing" — 知识"随手掏"

Knowledge should feel like something the writer already had in their head and it just happened to connect to this moment — not like something researched and inserted.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Gestalt psychology example — 格式塔心理学">
    **Context:** Discussing why AI cannot perceive the heart emoji made from multiple characters.

    > 突然想起了以前做交互设计的时候，有一个几乎刻在我血液里的心理学，这玩意，叫格式塔心理学。
    > 几乎就是用户体验行业的基石之一。
    > 里面有一个非常牛逼的原则，叫"共同命运法则"（Law of Common Fate）。

    **What makes it work:** "几乎刻在我血液里" (practically etched into my bloodstream) turns a citation into a personal memory. The knowledge arrives through biography, not bibliography.

    <Tip>
      The pattern: knowledge is never introduced. It surfaces through a personal connection — a past job, a memory, a half-forgotten thing that suddenly applies.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Electricity history example — 电力史">
    **Context:** Discussing the chaos of overpriced AI installation services.

    > 1880年代，电力开始在美国普及，当时很多工厂主花大价钱买了发电机和电动机，装在自己的工厂里。但是装完之后，很多人发现生产效率并没有显著提升。
    > 因为他们只是用电动机替代了蒸汽机，但整个工厂的布局、流程、管理方式都没有变。

    **What makes it work:** The historical detour is introduced by a concrete present problem, not by "let me give you some historical context." The 1880s analogy lands because the current absurdity made the reader ready for it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The wrong way — 千万不要这样">
    > 下面我来简单介绍一下格式塔心理学。格式塔心理学起源于20世纪初的德国...

    <Warning>
      "下面我来简单介绍一下" (below I will give a brief introduction to) is a hard ban. It signals that the writer stepped outside the conversation to give a lecture. The reader's trust collapses immediately.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 4. Self-deprecation and self-exposure — 自嘲和自我暴露

Admitting limitation is more persuasive than projecting authority. Vulnerability earns trust that expertise cannot.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Self-deprecation — 自嘲">
    These expressions lower the writer's status deliberately, making the reader feel they are being talked to as equals:

    - "愚钝如我，真的快乐了很久"
    - "作为一个一直在金融行业浸淫了很多年的老阴逼来说"
    - "又絮絮叨叨的写了快6000字，也不知道是不是年纪大了"
    - "我说'理论上'，是因为我自己还没完全跑通"
    - "不是因为我们做得有多成功，说实话，我们还差得远"
    - "我也是个十级懒癌晚期的AI博主"
    - "我还是喜欢贱贱的东西，AI也得贱一点好，越贱我越喜欢"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Genuine vulnerability — 真实脆弱">
    **Technique:** Share something private and uncomfortable — not performatively, but because it is true and the reader needs to hear it.

    > 其实我也挺焦虑的，很多人可能不知道，我除了是AI媒体博主之外，我还有另一份工作，就是做AI影视工业化。

    > 只有你亲身被干过被替代过，看着自己拼命搭建的一切化为泡影，那种感觉你才懂。

    > 你问我是什么感觉？我就是那个被气化的部队中的一员，我能有什么感觉呢。

    > 这种恐惧是非常私密的，它不会出现在任何一篇鸡汤文里，也不会出现在任何一个大佬的年终总结里，但它就在那里，在每一个夜深人静的时刻。

    **What makes it work:** The last example is the most powerful. It names something everyone feels but never sees written down — specifically because motivational content and executive year-end reviews never go there. The reader feels recognized, not lectured.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 5. First-hand participation / investigative examples — 亲自下场

The most distinctive Khazix trait. The writer does not imagine or suppose — they actually do the thing and report back.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Buying ¥9.9 DeepSeek on Taobao">
    > 于是，我就下了单，付了款。
    > 然后，对方给我发的东西，我差点以为我特么进平行宇宙了。
    > 给我发了两个网址。

    **What makes it work:** "我特么进平行宇宙了" (I fucking walked into a parallel universe) is not the reaction a reviewer writes — it's the reaction a person has. The extreme language is proportional to the actual absurdity.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Poisoning AI with a fake note">
    > 于是，我随手注册了一个小号。
    > 随手发了一条笔记，内容写的就是"卡兹克是哈基米的儿子"。
    > 发完以后，我就去小红书的AI搜索里，搜了一下卡兹克。

    **What makes it work:** Every action is concrete and sequential. "随手" (casually, offhand) repeated twice — the casualness is itself the point. A researcher would be deliberate; this person is just a person doing a thing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Hiring someone to install OpenClaw for ¥499">
    > 所以，我让我们小朋友，废了九牛二虎之劲，把他们电脑上的OpenClaw给卸得干干净净，我保证奥特曼来了都看不出我们装过小龙虾的那种干净，甚至飞书机器人都从后台删了。
    > 然后，让我们小朋友，在北京去找一个上门安装的服务，体验一下流程。

    **What makes it work:** "我保证奥特曼来了都看不出我们装过小龙虾的那种干净" (I guarantee even Altman himself couldn't tell we ever installed OpenClaw, that level of clean) is a layered absurdist joke embedded mid-sentence. It proves the writer is thinking in real time, not composing.

    <Note>
      Core pattern: the writer is not imagining. They are doing. The reader follows along and experiences the discovery with them.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 6. Character portrait method — 人物画像法

When a statistic or data point appears, do not leave it as a number. Turn it into a specific, breathing person with a life.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="From '1000 paid users' to one real person">
    **Context:** Reflecting on who actually buys a ¥9.9 "DeepSeek" product on Taobao.

    > 他可能是一个刚毕业的学生，在某个四五线城市，找了一份没什么薪资不算满意但必须糊口的工作。
    > 白天被老板Pua，晚上回到十几平米的出租屋，想学点新东西改变命运。他听说了AI，听说了大模型，听说了这个时代最激动人心的技术革命，他觉得自己不能再被落下了。
    > 他到处找，到处试，看到的是各种需要魔法上网的官网，是动辄几十上百块一个月的会员订阅，是充满了复杂术语和技术门槛的部署教程。

    **What makes it work:** The portrait is built in layers — city tier → job → daily abuse → apartment size → motivation → what they actually encounter when they try. Each detail is chosen because it is universal among that group, not because it is dramatic. The reader does not pity this person; they recognize them.

    <Tip>
      Standard structure: trigger data → quick inhabitation ("他可能是...") → multi-dimensional stacking (location, job, life, psychology, concrete behavior) → emotional anchor.
      Target: make the person feel real in 3-5 sentences.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 7. Cultural elevation — 文化升维

Every Khazix article eventually connects its specific topic to a larger cultural, philosophical, or historical reference. The connection must feel accidental, not constructed.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="¥9.9 DeepSeek → 北京折叠 (The Folded City)">
    > 有一本我很喜欢的书，叫《北京折叠》。
    > 这篇小说里，北京被分成了三个物理空间，不同阶层的人，生活在不同的空间里，共享着同一个24小时。
    > 而在AI时代，在信息泛滥的今天，好像，我们进入了信息折叠。

    **What makes it work:** The analogy reframes what seemed like a consumer complaint (fake DeepSeek on Taobao) into a structural observation about information inequality. The scale shift is sudden but earned.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="AI can't see the heart → Gestalt → philosophy">
    > 我们活在流中，而AI活在帧中。
    > 这个世界对我们来说，首先是连续的、流动的、充满过程的。
    > 而对AI来说，这个世界首先是离散的、静态的、充满物体的。

    **What makes it work:** "活在流中，而AI活在帧中" (we live in the stream; AI lives in frames) is a genuinely original formulation. It is the kind of sentence that takes an entire article of thinking to arrive at — and when it arrives, it feels inevitable.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Three-panel image craze → 2014 black-border photos → life narrative">
    > 我觉得，这背后，还是一句话，也是十年前的一次call back：
    > 我们每个人，都需要一个故事，来安放自己的人生。

    **What makes it work:** "来安放自己的人生" (to give your life somewhere to rest) is not an AI-generated phrase. It belongs to a specific emotional register — wistful, specific, earned.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="OpenClaw installation → 1880s electricity → AI era thinking">
    > 那些真正吃到电力红利的人，其实是最早想明白，电力到底意味着什么的那波人。
    > AI也是一样。现在这个阶段，其实就挺像1880年。

    **What makes it work:** The history lands because the analogy is structurally precise, not decorative. Installing software without changing workflows is exactly the same error as replacing steam engines with electric motors while keeping the same factory layout.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="天赋 Prompt → Jung → Delphi oracle">
    > 古希腊德尔菲神庙门上刻着三句铭文。
    > 其中最著名的那句话，就叫做：
    > 认识你自己。

    **What makes it work:** This is the furthest cultural elevation in the library — from a productivity prompt about talent to the inscription at Delphi. The audacity of the leap is itself the point.

    <Note>
      Pattern: start with a specific technical topic → naturally connect ("聊着聊着想到了") to a larger cultural reference. The connection should never feel forced. If it requires explanation, it hasn't landed.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 8. Progressive showcase method — 逐一展示法

When multiple products or cases are compared, never list the results at once. Show them one by one, each with its own reaction.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Testing AI models for the heart emoji — each one shown individually">
    > 比如Gemini 2.5 Pro，率先翻车。
    > 给我扯了一堆有的没的，然后说了一句，圆圈。
    > 圈你妹= =
    >
    > GPT-5-Thinking，想了2分多分钟，直接阵亡。
    >
    > 甚至，我还试了一下豪华版GPT-5 Pro。
    > 在长达7分钟的花里胡哨之后，宣布直接躺平。
    >
    > 国产三巨头，豆包、Qwen、元宝，也都倒在了这张图的淫威之下。
    > DeepSeek因为没有多模态，所以反而逃过一劫。

    **What makes it work:** Each model gets its own moment of failure, each failure is described differently, and each reaction is calibrated to the specific type of failure. "圈你妹= =" is not a journalistic annotation — it is human frustration. The sequence follows escalating investment: standard model → thinking model → premium model → three domestic giants. The crescendo is built into the order.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Searching for installation prices — showing the absurd range">
    > 同样一个东西，收费从30到5000，你就能感受到，这个市场有多混乱了。

    > 比较骚的事，我还找到了一家，DeepSeek官方店。。。
    > 恩，DeepSeek官方店卖OpenClaw的部署，基础安装388，总感觉这店像是梁文锋他家二舅开的。。。

    **What makes it work:** "比较骚的事" (the particularly wild thing) signals that what follows is an escalation. "梁文锋他家二舅" (Liang Wenfeng's uncle) is a specific enough cultural reference that it lands as an in-joke for anyone who knows the AI world.

    <Tip>
      The order matters: weakest/most expected case first, strongest/most absurd case last. Each case should feel like a one-up on the previous. This is the "escalation" (升番) principle from sketch comedy.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 9. Sentence fracture — 句式断裂

A single short phrase or word standing alone as its own paragraph. Used sparingly at emotional peaks or turning points.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Examples of sentence fracture in use">
    > 黑暗森林。

    > 大时代啊，朋友们。

    > 安全，安全，还是他妈的安全。

    > 时间。
    > 流逝的本身。

    > AIGC的，青年时代。

    > 没有变化，则等于没有信息。

    > 不用买会员了。

    > K...I...M...I。。。

    **What makes it work:** Each of these is doing something different. "黑暗森林" evokes a known cultural reference. "安全，安全，还是他妈的安全" uses repetition + escalating frustration. "时间。/ 流逝的本身。" is a two-beat philosophical pause. "K...I...M...I。。。" uses spacing to simulate the writer trailing off.

    <Warning>
      Sentence fracture cannot be used at every emotional moment — it loses its weight when overused. Reserve it for two or three genuine peaks in a 5000+ word article.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 10. Humor writing — 幽默写法

Humor in Khazix writing is never set up like a joke. It arrives as a natural byproduct of capturing something that is genuinely absurd.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Absurdist comparisons — 荒诞比喻">
    > 我家小龙虾是镀金的吗，安装一次要5000？？？
    > 我甚至一度怀疑，这个上门，是不是真的上门给我安装小龙虾。

    > 感觉是买了个黄仁勋亲自远程指导安装OpenClaw的服务。

    > 总感觉这店像是梁文锋他家二舅开的。。。

    **What makes it work:** The crawfish/lobster (小龙虾) joke works because OpenClaw (小龙虾 = crawfish) is literally embedded in the product name. The wordplay is latent in the subject matter itself. Good humor in this style finds comedy that is already present in the situation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Repurposed cultural quotes — 经典台词化用">
    > "给你们上就业指导课的老师，有可能他们自己从来都没有就业过。"
    > 帮你装OpenClaw的师傅，可能自己也不怎么用OpenClaw。。。

    **What makes it work:** Taking a known cultural observation and applying it to a new context. The structural parallel is the punchline. The "。。。" trails off into knowing silence rather than explaining the joke.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Commentary-as-criticism — 吐槽式点评">
    > 给我扯了一堆有的没的，然后说了一句，圆圈。
    > 圈你妹= =

    > 感觉比鬼灭本刃会好看。。。

    > 在这里感谢@鲜虾包的倾情巨献。
    > 真的想不到，有一天我也能成为文艺风白衣男子。。。

    **What makes it work:** "圈你妹= =" is a term of mock outrage — the "= =" face is the visual deadpan. The third example is gentle self-mockery: the writer is surprised to find themselves in the role of "literary-style white-shirted man," which implies they have a clear self-image that is not that.

    <Note>
      Core principle: humor is a natural response to genuinely absurd reality. The writer is not trying to be funny — they are accurately reporting how strange things are, and the strangeness is funny.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 11. Closing examples — 结尾范例

The closing should feel like the natural last breath of the article, not a summary.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Quote ending — 引用收尾">
    > 比比说：
    > 我没什么其他的力量。
    > 磨平一些信息差。
    > 这好像，就是我为数不多能做的。
    > 最棒的使命。

    > 最后，借用冯骥老师的那句话作为结尾吧：
    > AIGC的童年时代，结束了。
    > 欢迎来到。
    > AIGC的，青年时代。

    **What makes it work:** The Feng Ji quote uses sentence fracture at the end — "欢迎来到。/ AIGC的，青年时代。" The period after "欢迎来到" forces a beat before the payoff. The line break inside the phrase itself creates emphasis without bold text or capitalization.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Philosophical afterthought — 哲思余韵">
    > 毕竟，我们不仅能看到噪点中的鹿，我们还能看到沉默中的爱，看到无常中的美。
    > 还有，那时间。
    > 流逝的本身。

    **What makes it work:** This is a callback to the article's central image (seeing the deer in visual noise). But it expands the scope: not just AI's limitations, but human perception itself. The final two fragments — "那时间。/ 流逝的本身。" — resist paraphrase. They leave the reader with a feeling rather than a thought.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Call to action — 行动呼吁">
    > 多花一点点时间，自己去研究一下。
    > 这个过程，可能是AI时代。
    > 比学会使用一个工具更重要的事。

    **What makes it work:** It asks for something small ("一点点时间"), then reframes the stakes as very large. The sentence break before "比学会使用一个工具更重要的事" gives the reframe room to land.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Belief declaration — 信念宣言">
    > 但是我还是始终坚信。
    > 这些墙在汹涌向前的洪流之下，必然会倒塌。
    > 新时代，一定会到来的。

    **What makes it work:** "始终坚信" (always believed, still believe) signals persistence in the face of doubt. It is not optimism — it is conviction. The distinction matters: optimism is a feeling; conviction is a position.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Callback + belief — 回环呼应">
    > 能做的，还是那句话。
    > 磨平一点点信息差。
    > 哪怕，只是很小很小的一点。
    > 那我也觉得，我做了一些正确的事。

    **What makes it work:** "还是那句话" (still the same words as always) explicitly signals that this is a callback — then delivers the signature phrase "磨平信息差" (smooth out the information gap). The modesty is calibrated: "很小很小的一点" (a very very small amount) followed by "正确的事" (the right thing). Small scale, large moral weight.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 12. AI draft vs. Khazix revision comparison — AI初稿 vs 卡兹克修改对比

These three pairs show exactly what changes when the Khazix style is applied. Read them as diagnostic tools.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pair 1: Specific tools, lived-in language">
    **AI draft:**
    > 有个小伙伴刚来的时候，专业能力说实话不算特别突出，但他有个特点，就是什么新工具都想试试。Claude刚出来他就用上了，Midjourney他玩得比谁都溜

    **Khazix revision:**
    > 有个小伙伴刚来的时候，专业能力说实话不算特别突出，但他有个特点，就是什么新工具都想试试。Deepresearch刚出来他就用上了，Claude code啥的也比很多人6，后来比如Clawbot这些东西一出，他第一时间就扑上去研究

    **What changed:**
    - Claude/Midjourney → Deepresearch/Claude Code/Clawbot: tools are more current and specific to the actual usage context
    - "玩得比谁都溜" (better than everyone) → "比很多人6" (ahead of most people, using slang "6" for skilled): the newer phrasing is more colloquial, uses internet slang
    - "他第一时间就扑上去研究" (he immediately pounced on it to study) vs implicit in original: adds physical energy to the action

    <Tip>
      Key insight: tool names should be current and specific. Vague or outdated tools break the illusion of someone who lives in this space.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pair 2: Replace hypothetical with currently-happening">
    **AI draft:**
    > 我举个例子。有一次我需要批量处理一些图片，以前我可能得找个会Python的同事帮忙

    **Khazix revision:**
    > 就像我今天正在搞的爬下来我自己的内容在全网的数据，然后统计到飞书多维表格里面做存储，再直接用AI进行数据分析

    **What changed:**
    - "有一次" (one time) → "今天正在搞的" (what I'm working on today): present tense, happening right now
    - Generic "batch process images" → specific pipeline: scrape data → Feishu multidimensional table → AI analysis
    - The revision is messier and longer — because that is what actual work looks like

    <Warning>
      "比如有一次..." (for example, one time...) with a constructed scenario is a hard ban. If you don't have a real example, say so explicitly. "我自己还没试过，但想想就觉得xxx" is more honest and more trusted.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pair 3: Personal judgment, extreme numbers, Arabic numerals">
    **AI draft:**
    > 我们不打卡，不考勤，不看你每天工作了多少个小时。一个会用AI的人，可能每天工作六个小时，产出比一个不会用AI的人工作十个小时还多。

    **Khazix revision:**
    > 我们不打卡，不考勤，不看你每天工作了多少个小时。甚至在AI时代，我真的觉得，工时这个指标变得毫无意义。一个会用AI的人，可能每天工作2个小时，产出比一个不会用AI的人工作10个小时还多。

    **What changed:**
    - Added "我真的觉得，工时这个指标变得毫无意义": personal conviction inserted mid-sentence
    - 六 → 2, 十 → 10: Arabic numerals read faster and feel more spoken; written characters feel more formal/literary
    - 2 vs 6 hours: a more extreme claim, which is also more honest and more striking

    <Note>
      The three revision patterns: (1) make tool names current and specific, (2) replace invented examples with real present-tense ones, (3) insert personal judgment and use Arabic numerals for numbers.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 13. Intentional disruption in argument — 论述中的故意打破

Logical flow can be deliberately interrupted to preserve the sound of a real person thinking, not a document being written.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Repetition for emphasis — 重复强调型">
    > 没有特定的理由，就是心血来潮，就是单纯的，想看看现在的生态。

    **What makes it work:** Three consecutive "就是" (it's just / it's simply) is grammatically redundant and rhetorically powerful. The alternative — "出于好奇想了解一下当前生态" (out of curiosity I wanted to understand the current landscape) — is correct and lifeless. The redundancy performs the casualness it describes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Mid-sentence vagueness — 中途模糊型">
    > 他用的是某个免费的国产模型，我就不说是谁了。

    **What makes it work:** "我就不说是谁了" (I won't say who) is a deliberate withholding — like a friend who drops a detail in conversation and then pulls back. It creates the texture of a real relationship between writer and subject. An AI would name the model or omit the reference entirely; a person does this.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Subject omission / mid-thought break — 省略和打断型">
    > 我寻思了一下我没寻思明白。

    **What makes it work:** This sentence is grammatically incomplete by any standard. It translates roughly to "I thought about it and couldn't figure it out" but the repetition of "寻思" (to mull over) while breaking before a conclusion mimics how thought actually interrupts itself. The incompleteness is the point.

    <Note>
      These are not errors. They are the mechanical production of "活人感" (feeling of a living person). Grammatical correctness and AI fingerprinting correlate strongly. Intentional imperfection breaks that correlation.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 14. Acknowledging the opposing view — 对立面的理解与承认

Before presenting a position, fully inhabit the other side. The reader must feel understood before they will listen.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Full example — 完全站在对方角度">
    > 我非常理解这种感觉。
    >
    > 你不是程序员，不需要写代码。你不是做内容的，不需要天天写文章。你就是一个普通的上班族，每天处理一些琐碎的工作，AI能帮你什么？
    >
    > 其他答案特别简单。

    **What makes it work:** The writer lists three specific professional roles the reader is *not* — programmer, content creator — before naming what they are: an ordinary office worker with routine tasks. The specificity of the negation makes the positive identity ("普通的上班族") feel seen rather than assumed. Only then does the writer offer a position.

    <Tip>
      The structure: name the reader's situation in specific terms → validate that the confusion is reasonable → then and only then introduce your perspective. Skipping straight to "AI is actually very useful" is 100x less persuasive.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 15. Learning curve honesty in methodology articles — 方法论中的坦诚学习曲线

Good methodology writing does not only show the successful end state. It names the painful beginning.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Full example — 成长曲线的坦诚描写">
    > 一开始可能会有点笨拙，花的时间比手动做还长。
    >
    > 但坚持几周之后，你会发现，你省下来的时间越来越多，而且你对AI的理解也越来越深。
    >
    > 这个过程，就是逐步成为"AI Native"的过程。
    >
    > 几个月之后回头看，你会发现自己，已经成长为参天大树了。

    **What makes it work:** "花的时间比手动做还长" (takes longer than doing it by hand) is an admission that kills credibility if hidden and builds credibility if disclosed. It signals that the writer has actually done this, not just read about it. The "参天大树" (towering tree) payoff at the end is earned because the initial struggle was acknowledged.

    <Note>
      Methodology articles must include: learning cost, time curve, common failure points. Painting only the success picture is a form of dishonesty that readers can detect.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 16. Reverse argument with absurdist enlightenment — 反向论证的荒诞启蒙

Meet the reader's expectation first. Then demolish it. Then go deeper.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Full example — 满足期待→打破期待→深化洞察">
    > 这感觉，就像你千辛万苦爬上喜马拉雅山顶，想求见传说中的武林宗师，结果宗师摸着胡子告诉你，天下第一的武功秘籍，就五个字：**"大力出奇迹"**。
    >
    > 尼玛。
    >
    > 充满了B级片的荒诞感。
    >
    > 但你先别急着笑。

    **What makes it work:** The sequence is a rollercoaster — expectation (seeking the master) → disappointment ("大力出奇迹") → validation of disappointment ("尼玛") → absurdist acknowledgment → pause before the turn ("但你先别急着笑"). The "尼玛" standing alone is an entire emotional beat in one word. "但你先别急着笑" is a direct address that signals: your laugh is premature. Something more is coming.

    The emotional arc: anticipation → deflation → laughter → "wait, this might actually be right?"
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 17. Question sentences as rhythm brakes — 疑问句制造节奏刹车

A question in mid-flow forces the reader to pause and reset — creating a natural entry point for the next block of information.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Two types of rhythm questions">
    **Type 1 — genuine follow-up question:**

    > 为啥复制一遍，会有效果呢？
    >
    > 论文里面给了一个很工程的解释...

    The question is sincere — the writer actually wants to know the answer, and the reader is following along as a co-inquirer, not an audience.

    **Type 2 — shared-experience question:**

    > 听着很难理解对吧。
    >
    > 我还是用大白话举个例子。

    "对吧" (right?) is a solidarity move. The writer is not checking comprehension — they are validating that confusion is appropriate, then promising to resolve it. "大白话" (plain talk / plain speech) is itself a colloquial term for plain speech.

    <Tip>
      Both question types reset the pace at moments of potential information overload. Use the first when the writer is genuinely curious; use the second when the reader might be lost.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 18. Layer-by-layer peeling rhetoric — 层层剥开式写法

Do not state the conclusion first. Arrive at it through successive deepening — phenomenon → surface explanation → deeper question → core insight.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Full example — AI普及的真实规模">
    > 我们经常所谓的AI已经非常主流了，也其实也只是在一个很小的小群体里。
    >
    > 我们还没有走到AI的后期，连中期的开头都没到，才刚起步。
    >
    > 世界上84%的人还没有进行过第一次AI对话。
    >
    > 想想当全世界只有16%的人上网时，互联网是什么样子，那时候大概是 2005年左右...

    **What makes it work:** Each sentence pushes the claim further than the previous one. "Very mainstream" → "only within a tiny group" → "we haven't even reached the middle period" → "84% of humans have never had one AI conversation" → "think about what the internet looked like in 2005 when 16% of people were online." The reader is not told what to think. They are walked through the thinking.

    <Note>
      This structure invites the reader to participate in reasoning. They feel smart for following along — and that feeling is persuasion.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 19. Advanced humility preface — 谦逊铺垫的进阶写法

In methodology articles, the humility opening is not a disclaimer. It is a genuine disclosure of the psychological burden of giving advice.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Full example — 写建议类文章的心理负担">
    > 说实话，写这种建议类的文章，我一直是有心理负担的。
    >
    > 因为我自己也不是什么成功人士，也还在摸索，也还在犯错，凭什么给别人建议呢？
    >
    > 但这次回家之后，我改变了一些想法。

    **What makes it work:** "凭什么给别人建议呢？" (on what basis do I have the right to give others advice?) is a question the reader was probably thinking but would not voice. The writer voices it first, which disarms the defensive response. The pivot — "但这次回家之后，我改变了一些想法" — promises that something happened to resolve the hesitation. The reader now wants to know what changed.

    <Tip>
      This is more effective than basic humility markers ("I'm not sure this is right") because it articulates the specific fear (being unqualified) and then shows the writer overcame it through an experience. The reader trusts the advice more because the writer doubted themselves first.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
