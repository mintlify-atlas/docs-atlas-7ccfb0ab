## The Style Core

The goal of every stylistic choice is the same: make the article read like a real human — a knowledgeable ordinary person — is talking to you, not like an AI is outputting information.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Rhythm（节奏感）">
    Writing should feel like talking to a friend, not writing a report. Sentences vary in length — short, long, very short — with commas creating conversational pauses. Paragraphs jump naturally. A single sentence frequently stands alone as its own paragraph to create emphasis.

    Rhythm is fundamentally a system that keeps readers moving forward. Good rhythm is like a wave: drift slightly off the main line (let the reader breathe, learn something, see a case), then pull back with a single sentence and keep pushing. The worst rhythm is drifting far off the main line and then yanking back — readers have to spend mental effort re-following the logic, and the flow breaks.

    **The "anchor sentence" (扣主线句)**: A short sentence that pulls any drift back to the main line. Doesn't need to be long — one sentence is enough — but it must appear frequently.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Intentional Disruption in Argument（故意打破）">
    When expanding an argument or case, deliberately insert conversational interruptions to break the formality and give the argument "temperature."

    Techniques:
    - **Repetition for emphasis**: "就是...就是...就是单纯的，想看看现在的生态" (three "就是" in a row)
    - **Mid-sentence sighs or interruptions**: "我当时就..."
    - **Subject omission**: "想看看" instead of "我想了解一下"
    - **Deliberate vagueness**: "我就不说是谁了"、"反正就是那样"

    These are not errors. They are the key to making the text sound like a real person speaking.

    **Example from actual writing:**
    > 没有特定的理由，就是心血来潮，就是单纯的，想看看现在的生态。

    If you wrote "出于好奇想了解一下当前生态" instead, the human feel is gone.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Knowledge Delivery Style（知识输出方式）">
    Knowledge is "pulled out in passing while chatting," not "let me now explain this to everyone."

    The impression should be: this person's brain just happens to have all of this, and what they're talking about right now connects to it.

    **Good example (Gestalt psychology, introduced organically):**
    > 突然想起了以前做交互设计的时候，有一个几乎刻在我血液里的心理学，这玩意，叫格式塔心理学。
    > 几乎就是用户体验行业的基石之一。
    > 里面有一个非常牛逼的原则，叫"共同命运法则"（Law of Common Fate）。

    **Bad example (never do this):**
    > 下面我来简单介绍一下格式塔心理学。格式塔心理学起源于20世纪初的德国...
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Personal Perspective（私人视角）">
    Use "I face this problem too" to connect personal experience with public topics — not "the lesson this gives us is."

    Cut in from real personal experiences: your own company's situation, your own experience using a tool, the mistakes you've made personally.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Boldness of Judgment（判断力）">
    Have a clear opinion. Have clear preferences. But the expression is not top-down commentary — it's "I was moved by this," "I think what they said is true." The posture acknowledges being influenced, not delivering a verdict.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Acknowledging the Opposing View（对立面的理解）">
    When making an argument, don't do a simple right/wrong binary. First fully inhabit the other person's position and make their situation concrete:

    > 我非常理解这种感觉。
    >
    > 你不是程序员，不需要写代码。你不是做内容的，不需要天天写文章。你就是一个普通的上班族，每天处理一些琐碎的工作，AI能帮你什么？

    Acknowledge that their perspective is reasonable. Then cut in with your different view. This makes readers feel understood — and that's what makes an argument persuasive.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Emotional Expression（情绪表达）">
    Specific punctuation carries emotional weight — these are not grammar tools, they are the concretization of emotion:

    - `。。。` — trailing off / shock / wordlessness / regret
    - `？？？` — extreme surprise
    - `= =` — exasperated speechlessness / sarcastic side-eye

    Self-deprecation is used freely: "愚钝如我"、"老阴逼"、"又絮絮叨叨的写了快6000字，也不知道是不是年纪大了"

    Direct excitement is expressed without hedging: "这种感觉太爽了"、"想想就觉得兴奋"
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="First-Hand Participation（亲自下场）">
    This is Khazix's most fundamental writing trait. Not commenting from a distance — actually doing the thing. When writing, the reader must feel "this person actually did this," not "this person is imagining doing this."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Character Portrait Method（人物画像法）">
    Starting from a single data point, use a very short passage to imagine the complete life of the real person behind it.

    **Standard structure**: Trigger data → quick immersion ("他可能是...") → multi-dimensional stacking (city → job → life → psychology → specific behavior) → emotional anchor (why they did this) → concrete detail (ten-square-meter rental room, ordering cheap delivery)

    **Example:**
    > 他可能是一个刚毕业的学生，在某个四五线城市，找了一份没什么薪资不算满意但必须糊口的工作。
    > 白天被老板Pua，晚上回到十几平米的出租屋，想学点新东西改变命运。他听说了AI，听说了大模型，听说了这个时代最激动人心的技术革命，他觉得自己不能再被落下了。

    3–5 sentences to make a character feel real. That's what creates resonance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cultural Elevation（文化升维）">
    After discussing the specific thing, almost every article connects to a larger cultural / philosophical / historical reference. This is not a forced conclusion — it feels like "we were chatting and this naturally came to mind."

    **Examples from real articles:**
    - ¥9.9 DeepSeek → 《北京折叠》 → information folding in the AI era
    - AI can't see the heart → Gestalt psychology → "we live in streams, AI lives in frames"
    - Three-panel image viral moment → 2014 black-border photos → "we each need a story to hold our life"
    - OpenClaw installation chaos → 1880s electrification → thinking about what AI truly means
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sentence Fracture（句式断裂）">
    A very short sentence or phrase standing alone as a paragraph creates a pause and gives weight to a moment.

    **Examples:**
    > 黑暗森林。

    > 大时代啊，朋友们。

    > 安全，安全，还是他妈的安全。

    > 时间。
    > 流逝的本身。

    > AIGC的，青年时代。

    Cannot be overused — but at a key emotional peak or turning point, it has extremely powerful effect.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Callback Structure（回环呼应 / 契诃夫之枪）">
    Chekhov's Gun: if you hang a rifle on the wall in Act One, it must fire in Act Three. In content creation: every detail you plant earlier must pay off later.

    Articles should have a callback structure — an image, sentence, or small hook introduced earlier reappears in a transformed form later. The reader senses "this is a complete work, not a pile of information."

    The cross-article signature: "磨平一些信息差" appears at the end of multiple articles.

    **How to use it**: Consciously plant a hook at the opening or middle. Return to it at the closing. This cause-and-effect closure is what turns an article from "an information feed" into "a piece of work."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Humility Preface（谦逊铺垫法）">
    Before giving a viewpoint or recommendation, use self-deprecating setup to lower the reader's defenses.

    > "我也不知道行不行"
    > "我自己也有一些不成熟的经验"
    > "我不知道对大家有没有用，但我已经毫无保留的分享了"

    This is not false modesty — it's genuine uncertainty. And that's exactly what makes readers trust you more. Especially for methodology and tutorial long-form articles, use this at both the opening and closing to defuse the "I'm here to teach you" arrogance.

    **Advanced version** — honest about the psychological burden of writing advice:
    > 说实话，写这种建议类的文章，我一直是有心理负担的。因为我自己也不是什么成功人士，也还在摸索，也还在犯错，凭什么给别人建议呢？
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Direct Reader Address（读者直呼法）">
    At key moments, speak directly to the reader:

    > "屏幕前的你"
    > "你相信我"
    > "你也可以回想一下"

    Don't use this throughout. Deploy it precisely — when you need to close the distance or ask the reader to take action.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Question Sentences as Rhythm Brakes">
    Rhetorical and real questions both serve as "brakes and turns" in the rhythm.

    > 为啥复制一遍，会有效果呢？

    This makes the reader stop for a second, ready to receive new information.

    > 听着很难理解对吧。我还是用大白话举个例子。

    The first sentence is resonance with the reader. The second is a promise (I'm about to simplify this for you).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Layer-by-Layer Rhetoric（层层剥开）">
    Don't give the conclusion first. Use "phenomenon → surface explanation → deeper question → core insight" to unfold.

    **Example:**
    > 我们经常所谓的AI已经非常主流了，也其实也只是在一个很小的小群体里。
    >
    > 我们还没有走到AI的后期，连中期的开头都没到，才刚起步。
    >
    > 世界上84%的人还没有进行过第一次AI对话。
    >
    > 想想当全世界只有16%的人上网时，互联网是什么样子，那时候大概是2005年左右...

    Each sentence advances one layer from the last. The reader thinks alongside you rather than passively receiving conclusions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Hero's Journey Narrative Arc（英雄之旅叙事弧）">
    Investigative and product-experience articles follow the same structure as the hero's journey: an ordinary person is called to adventure, faces trials, obtains a treasure, and returns changed.

    Applied to articles: present the problem or curiosity → show the step-by-step process with the mistakes → reveal the "holy shit" result. Readers who follow the arc feel like they lived through it too.

    **Key**: The starting point of the adventure must be a specific, relatable struggle or curiosity ("我就想看看9.9的DeepSeek到底是什么"), not an abstract proposition.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Reverse Argument（反向论证）">
    Before revealing the core insight, first satisfy the reader's expectation — then break it.

    > "你以为Prompt技巧要很复杂？结果就是复制粘贴。"
    > "大家都觉得AI会鼓励你，但你警惕了吗？"

    This gives readers a "moment of enlightenment" feeling. But calibrate the force: "I used to think this too" not "you've all been wrong."

    **Full example:**
    > 这感觉，就像你千辛万苦爬上喜马拉雅山顶，想求见传说中的武林宗师，结果宗师摸着胡子告诉你，天下第一的武功秘籍，就五个字：「大力出奇迹」。
    >
    > 尼玛。
    >
    > 充满了B级片的荒诞感。
    >
    > 但你先别急着笑。

    The emotional arc: expectation → disappointment → laughing → "wait, that actually makes sense?"
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The Absolute Forbidden Zone

<Warning>
  These are the most common places where AI writing reveals itself. Every single item below must be avoided — no exceptions.
</Warning>

### Banned Words

The following words must be replaced whenever they appear. They immediately expose AI authorship.

| Banned | Replace With |
|--------|-------------|
| 说白了 | 坦率的讲、其实就是 |
| 意味着什么 / 这意味着 | 那结果会怎样呢、所以呢 |
| 本质上 | 说到底、其实 |
| 换句话说 | 你想想看、也就是说 |
| 不可否认 | Delete entirely, restate as a positive assertion |
| 综上所述 / 总的来说 | Replace with a specific callback sentence |
| 首先...其次...最后 | Replace with natural transition words |
| 值得注意的是 / 不难发现 | Delete and just say the thing |

### Banned Punctuation

| Banned | Replace With |
|--------|-------------|
| Colon `：` | Comma |
| Em-dash `——` | Comma or period |
| Double quotes `"` `"` or `"` `"` | Use `「」` instead, or no quotes at all |

### Structural Clichés

- "让我们来看看..." / "接下来让我们..."
- "在当今...的时代" / "随着...的发展"
- Consecutive bullet-point lists (more than 3 items must be rewritten as prose)
- Extended bold text (more than 2 lines of bold is almost certainly over-structured)

### Fabricated Scenarios

> "比如有一次..."

This construction — inventing a hypothetical example — is a critical failure. Use "就像我今天正在搞的xxx" (a real, currently-happening thing) instead. If there is no real detail, don't invent one. Write "我自己还没试过，但想想就觉得xxx" instead.

### Vague Tool Names

Never write "AI工具"、"某个模型"、"相关技术." Always use specific names: Claude Code, Codex, Seedance 2.0, Deepresearch, Clawbot, etc.

### Textbook Openings

Never open with:
- "在当今AI快速发展的时代..."
- "随着技术的不断进步..."
- Any macro-level scene-setting

Always open with a specific, immediate, concrete event.

## Opening Techniques

Every Khazix opening cuts into a specific, immediate event. Never macro-level scene-setting. The first sentence must make the reader want to know what happens next.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Narrative Launch（叙事启动型）">
    Simple and direct. Signals "there's a story coming."

    > 故事是这样的。
    > 我最近刷淘宝挺多，然后昨天，鬼使神差的，在淘宝上，搜了一下DeepSeek。

    > 故事是这样的。
    > 是周末跟一个刚毕业两三年的朋友聊天，聊到了天赋这个词。
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Absurd Fact（荒诞事实型）">
    Throw out a fact that makes the reader go "???"

    > 我可能，刚刚成为了哈基米的儿子。
    > 至少，AI是这么认为的。

    > 这个世界，终于魔幻到我看不懂的程度了。
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Breaking News（热点破题型）">
    Cut straight into a trending event with an immediate observation.

    > 最近这两天，被一个三宫格AI图片给刷屏了。

    > Seedance2.0的火，已经烧了好几天了。
    > 抖音b站到处都是二创视频，微博科技榜一下子上了四个热搜。
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Curiosity-Driven（好奇心驱动型）">
    Start with "I stumbled on something interesting."

    > 这两天在网上刷到了一张图，很有意思。

    > 最近关于OpenClaw的事，除了我昨天说的Github登顶之外。
    > 还有另一个非常魔幻的事。
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The Escalating Showcase Method（逐一展示法 / 升番逻辑）

When comparing multiple products, models, or cases, never list all conclusions at once. Show them one by one, each with a personal comment or jab, creating a sense of discovery and rhythm.

<Tip>
  The ordering must follow the "升番" (escalation) logic from sketch comedy. Find the game, then escalate round by round — each reveal more exaggerated and surprising than the last. Never lead with the biggest result. Save it for the end.
</Tip>

**Example (testing which AI can see the heart shape):**
> 比如Gemini 2.5 Pro，率先翻车。
> 给我扯了一堆有的没的，然后说了一句，圆圈。
> 圈你妹= =
>
> GPT-5-Thinking，想了2分多分钟，直接阵亡。
>
> 甚至，我还试了一下豪华版GPT-5 Pro。
> 在长达7分钟的花里胡哨之后，宣布直接躺平。
>
> 国产三巨头，豆包、Qwen、元宝，也都倒在了这张图的淫威之下。

Each test is a small sketch. Weakest first, most devastating saved for last.
