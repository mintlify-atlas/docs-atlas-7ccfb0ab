### Step 1: Understand the Material and Evaluate the Topic

Users may provide input in any form: a product brief, a news link, a PDF, a voice-to-text transcript, scattered notes, or an opening paragraph plus a few bullet points.

**Digest the material first, then assess topic quality.**

#### The HKR Quality Check

Every topic must pass the HKR test before you start writing:

| Grade | H (Happy / Interesting) | K (Knowledge / Informative) | R (Resonance / Emotional) |
|-------|------------------------|----------------------------|--------------------------|
| **S-tier** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| **Passing** | Any two of three | | |
| **Needs rework** | Only one | | |
| **Reject** | None | | |

- **H (Happy)**: Is it interesting enough? Does it have suspense? Would a title and opening make someone curious enough to click?
- **K (Knowledge)**: Is it informative? Will readers learn something new by the end?
- **R (Resonance)**: Does it hit an emotion? Will readers feel "yes, exactly, that's what I think too"?

<Info>
  If the topic clearly scores only one item or none, proactively tell the user and suggest adjusting the direction before writing.
</Info>

#### What to Do If the Material Is Thin

If you only have a topic but no specific points, ask the user for more before proceeding:

> 你大概想讲哪几个点？有没有什么自己的经历想放进去？有没有什么让你特别兴奋或者特别想吐槽的地方？

This is not optional. Thin material produces generic articles that lack the personal texture that makes Khazix's writing work.

### Step 2: Define AI's Role Boundaries

This step is critical. This skill is a style generator, not a thinking replacement. **The highest value AI provides is material and inspiration — not generating content wholesale.**

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="What AI Is Good At">
    **Finding evidence and proof**
    Give AI an angle, and it can find supporting (and opposing) arguments from history, academia, and culture. If you want to express "information gaps are eternal," AI can surface 《北京折叠》, cyberpunk's "High Tech, Low Life," and the 1880s electrification of American factories.

    **Finding analogies and metaphors**
    When you need a vivid metaphor for an abstract concept, AI can provide multiple candidates. If you already have one ("AI is like an all-capable intern"), give it to AI and tell it to write with that framing. If you don't, let AI offer options and you pick.

    **Expanding on a confirmed angle**
    When you've decided on the core angle and have rough section headings, AI can fill in the arguments and details. Once you've committed to "information is folded into three layers" as the frame, and written the heading for each layer, AI can expand the content of each layer.

    **Filling in academic or disciplinary background**
    Gestalt psychology, Jungian shadow theory, causal language model principles — AI can accurately articulate these so you don't have to look them up.

    **Suggesting logic and structure**
    If you're unsure where a paragraph fits, or a section feels logically loose, AI can help restructure.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="What Humans Must Do">
    **First-hand observations and real experiences**
    Buying the ¥9.9 DeepSeek. Paying ¥499 for someone to come install OpenClaw. Sneaking out at 3am to use an internet café. These cannot be fabricated — the moment they are, readers sense it.

    **The core creative angle**
    Connecting "Taobao sells DeepSeek" to 《北京折叠》. Deriving "we live in streams, AI lives in frames" from "AI can't see the heart." These are the pivots that make an article stand up. AI can offer many candidates, but the "yes, that's it" judgment must be yours.

    **Authentic emotional expression**
    "我当时就愣住了" (I was stunned right then) not "我当时很震撼" (I was very moved at the time). The first is a body-memory; the second is a descriptive label. AI defaults to the second. You must write the first.

    **The empathy bridge from data to people**
    From "1,000 paid orders" — imagining the complete life of that newly graduated student in a fourth-tier city. That warmth requires the author to genuinely feel it.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

#### The Ideal Collaboration Workflow

```
Human: source material + core viewpoint + personal experience + emotional nodes
  ↓
AI: background knowledge + evidence/analogies + structure suggestions + angle expansion
  ↓
Human: second-pass rewrite (add personal voice, break rhythm, inject real details)
  ↓
AI: run four-layer self-check → output specific improvement suggestions
  ↓
Human: final review and approval
```

### Step 3: Write the Article

#### The Five Article Archetypes

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Investigative-Experiment Type（调查实验型）">
    **Core premise**: "I did this for you."

    The writer personally goes and does the thing — buys the ¥9.9 DeepSeek, plants fake notes to poison an AI, hires someone to install OpenClaw — and then reports what they found.

    **Writing focus**: Process narrative and layered discovery. Every step brings a new finding, and every finding carries a real reaction.

    **Structure**: Hero's journey arc — a specific, relatable problem or curiosity as the starting point → step-by-step doing, with mishaps → the "holy shit" result.

    **Key test (L3-5)**: Does the article have the feel of "I was actually there"? Does the process reveal things in layers?
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="2. Product-Experience Type（产品体验型）">
    **Core premise**: "Come play with me."

    The writer gets access to the product, uses it for real, and brings the reader along. This is not a review report — it's a shared experience.

    **Writing focus**: Scene demonstration and authentic reactions. Show the actual usage context. Include natural comparisons to other products that come up organically.

    **Key test (L3-5)**: Is there a real usage scenario? Are there natural comparisons (not forced) to other products?
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="3. Phenomenon-Analysis Type（现象解读型）">
    **Core premise**: "Did you notice this? What's behind it?"

    The writer observes something interesting — a three-panel AI image going viral, AI unable to see a heart shape — and then digs deeper and deeper.

    **Writing focus**: Curiosity-driven progression: observation → curiosity → research → philosophical elevation. The knowledge emerges naturally from the investigation, not as pre-planned exposition.

    **Key test (L3-5)**: Does the article follow "observation → curiosity → research → elevation"? Does the cultural/philosophical connection feel organic?
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="4. Tool-Sharing Type（工具分享型）">
    **Core premise**: "I found something good."

    The writer shares a useful tool or Prompt, but wraps it in a personal story. Never open with "here's a tool I recommend."

    **Writing focus**: Personal story setup (what problem were you facing? what made you go looking?) → natural introduction of the tool → your own usage process → the wow-factor result. The "卧槽" (holy shit) moment at the end should feel genuinely surprising.

    **Key test (L3-5)**: Is there a personal story setup? Does the result demonstration produce a genuine "wow" reaction?
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="5. Methodology-Sharing Type（方法论分享型）">
    **Core premise**: "I'm handing you my best stuff."

    The writer systematically shares long-accumulated experience and methodology.

    **Writing focus**:
    - Every section must give the reader one action they can take today
    - Structure: viewpoint → case/theory support → specifically what to do → honest acknowledgment of learning curve and failure points
    - Humility preface at the opening and closing to defuse the "let me teach you" arrogance
    - Closing returns to all action points and elevates

    **Example of the humility preface**:
    > 说实话，写这种建议类的文章，我一直是有心理负担的。因为我自己也不是什么成功人士，也还在摸索，也还在犯错，凭什么给别人建议呢？

    **Example of the honest learning curve**:
    > 一开始可能会有点笨拙，花的时间比手动做还长。但坚持几周之后，你会发现，你省下来的时间越来越多，而且你对AI的理解也越来越深。

    **Key test (L3-5)**: Does every section end with an actionable step? Does it honestly acknowledge the learning cost and failure points? Is there a humility preface?
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

#### Key Structural Template

```
【Opening】
Sensory/emotional hook — start from one specific, immediate event or scene.
Build emotion fast.
  ↓
【Background】
Brief, conversational context-setting. Let non-expert readers follow along.
Don't lecture. Deliver knowledge like you're "just mentioning it."
  ↓
【Core Content】
Several sections, each containing:
  - One clear viewpoint
  - At least one concrete scene / person / dialogue to support it
  - A personal connection ("I face this too")
  - An "anchor sentence" (扣主线句) that pulls any drift back to the main line
  ↓
【Elevation】
Connect the specific event to a larger cultural / philosophical / historical reference.
Not a thesis conclusion — more like "I was talking about this and suddenly thought of..."
  ↓
【Closing】
Choose the most fitting ending type:
  - Quote ending: close with someone else's words ("比比说：磨平一些信息差")
  - Philosophical resonance: a short sentence with space to breathe ("时间。流逝的本身。")
  - Call to action: encourage the reader to do one thing
  - Belief declaration: express conviction about the future
  - Callback: return to the opening image, but the viewpoint has shifted
  ↓
【Fixed Footer】
以上，既然看到这里了，如果觉得不错，随手点个赞、在看、转发三连吧，
如果想第一时间收到推送，也可以给我个星标⭐～
谢谢你看我的文章，我们，下次再见。
> / 作者：卡兹克
> / 投稿或爆料，请联系邮箱：wzglyay@virxact.com
```

#### Word Count and Format Guidelines

<Tip>
  These are guidelines, not rigid rules. Let the content determine the length. Never pad to hit a word count; never cut a line that matters.
</Tip>

- **Length**: 4,000–8,000 characters for a typical long-form article
- **Paragraphs**: Short. Many paragraphs are a single sentence. Important statements get their own line with breathing room before and after.
- **Subheadings**: Almost never. The vast majority of articles flow from start to finish without any subheadings, relying on rhythm and colloquial transitions to move between sections. The only exception is a "N points / N lessons" methodology article, which can use numbered items (1、2、3) in the body text — but these are not markdown headings, just inline numbers.
- **Bold**: Use sparingly. More than two lines of bold in a row is almost certainly over-structured.
- **Transitions**: Use colloquial phrases, not structural markers: "说到这个"、"回到xxx这块"、"顺着上面的再聊聊"

### Step 4: Run the Four-Layer Self-Check

After finishing a draft, run the complete four-layer quality check before delivering the article. This is not optional.

<Note>
  See the [Quality Checklist](/khazix-writer/quality-checklist) page for the complete self-check system with all specific criteria and the output report format.
</Note>

The four layers, from most mechanical to most subjective:

| Layer | Type | What It Checks |
|-------|------|---------------|
| **L1** | Hard Rules (auto-scan) | Banned words, banned punctuation, structural clichés, vague tool names |
| **L2** | Style Consistency (pattern matching) | Opening, rhythm, colloquial expressions, punctuation prohibition |
| **L3** | Content Quality (deep review) | Argument support, knowledge delivery style, cultural elevation, empathy for opposing views, archetype-specific checks |
| **L4** | Human Feel (personality layer) | Warmth, uniqueness, posture, flow |

All four layers must pass. The article is not done until they do.
